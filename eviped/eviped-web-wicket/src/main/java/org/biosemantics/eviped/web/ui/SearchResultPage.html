Project Details

Within the context of a recently funded ZONMW project (EVIPED) funded under the Priority of Medicines for Children call and the FP-7 (EC)funded Global Research Initiative for Pediatrics (GRIP), we aim at building and using infrastructure to study the effects of drugs in children by secondary use of data generated in routine care. This is done, based on the concept that millions of children are treated with drugs on a daily basis and we should actually learn from their experiences rather than complaining we have no adequate data in paediatrics. This research will be carried out at the department of Medical Informatics in close collaboration with the Sophia Childrenâ€™s Hospital of the Erasmus MC.

For these positions we require a person to have good computer, analytical, writing and social skills and to be convinced that we could learn from routine care data to improve paediatric drug prescribing. Preferably the candidate is a medical doctor or pharmacist with interest in (Pharmaco)epidemiology but candidates with alternative quantitatively oriented prior degrees may be considered. Specific experience and/or interest in paediatrics is an asset. The candidate should be able to work independently in a multi-disciplinary team and to have interest in liaising and working in collaboration with many different partners locally and internationally.

The department of Medical Informatics is specialized in using routine care data to study the effects of drugs. Large expertise has been built in using GP medical record data on more than a million patients in the Netherlands (the IPCI database: http://www.ipci.nl). Currently the department is coordinating and collaborating in several projects that aim at building EU wide linkage of database to increase the scale of drug and vaccine safety investigations (www.euadr-project.org; http://www. sos-nsaids-projetc.org; http://www.aritmo-project.org; http://www.vaesco.net).
